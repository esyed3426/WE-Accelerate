This is a review of callbacks, promises, and async/await syntax.

Callbacks, promises, and async/await are methods to deal with asynchronous data. 

Asynchronous programming is a technique that enables your program to start
a potentially lengthy task, and rather than having to wait until that task is finished, to
continue to respond to other events while that task runs. You can move to another task before
the previous one is completed.

Callbacks were widely used until the release of ES6 when promises were introduced
